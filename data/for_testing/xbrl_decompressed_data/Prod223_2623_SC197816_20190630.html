Strategic Report
for the Year Ended 30 June 2019

The directors present their strategic report for the year ended 30 June 2019.

Strategic Management

The last financial year saw a year of consolidation for the company. After 3 major acquisitions at the tail end
of the 2018 financial year, the management team focused its efforts on securing the overall trading
performance of the company.

The acquisitions at the end of the 2018 financial year were all for managed units with one being freehold and
the other 2 being leasehold. This is in line with the company's strategy to operate freehold properties but also
to look at leasehold properties where the length of the lease and the projected returns are sufficient to build
value.

The focus of the company remains in Edinburgh's Old Town, where it benefits from a mixture of loyal local
trade and a brisk tourist trade.

Although there were no further acquisitions made during the year, the directors have been working on a
number of exciting development projects. One of these is expected to be trading in the second half of the
calendar year 2020. In addition to the development projects, a "marquee" acquisition was identified in 2019
and this is expected to complete during 2020.

The last 2 major redevelopment projects which the company has undertaken have helped demonstrate the
credentials of the management team in this area. Stramash (redeveloped 2015/16) and George IV
(redeveloped 2017/18) have been growing year on year with these two units now contributing 31% of the
company turnover.

Business Environment

The business is still predominantly focused on the Old Town area of Edinburgh serving both locals and
tourists and this will remain a key trading area. Expansion into other geographic areas will be considered
where acquisitions can be identified that will provide a suitable return and add value to the company.

REVIEW OF BUSINESS
The results for the year and financial position of the company are as shown in the annexed financial
statements.

The company grew turnover to £11,446,188 from £7,472,664 (2018). The new units acquired at the end of the
2018 financial year end contributed £1,962,840 of this growth. The current financial year also benefited from
a full year trading of the refurbished George IV. This has proved to be an extremely popular local venue and
sales were up £1,090,506 on the prior year. The unit was closed for refurbishment for part of the prior year

The profit before tax for the year amounted to £535,296 (profit in 2018: £388,101).

The company generated cash from operations (before tax and interest) of £1,433,650 which was an increase
of £534,177 on the prior year of £899,473. EBITDA for the year ending 30th June 2019 was £1,165,877 (2018
: £729,022).

PRINCIPAL RISKS AND UNCERTAINTIES
As at the date of signing, the Coronavirus outbreak is causing considerable uncertainty for all businesses in
the UK, but particularly those in the leisure sector.The directors have taken immediate measures to reduce
costs and preserve cash. Although they expect income and profits to be significantly down for the remainder
of the financial year ending 30th June 2020, they anticipate that the business will recover once the worst of
the outbreak is over. If measures to combat the outbreak were to continue into the Edinburgh Festival
season, then this would have a detrimental impact to earnings at the start of the next financial year. However,
the directors anticipate that although this would be an unwelcome development the measures being taken
and ongoing support from the company's bankers would allow it to trade through it.

The directors have taken the following measures in order to preserve cash and safeguard the company:-

- Staff have been furloughed as of 23rd March 2020 and the company will be making application to the
government job retention scheme when this becomes available.
- The company will benefit from the reduction in property rates for the rates year 2020/21. This will start to
provide an immediate benefit as rates payments due in April will no longer have to be made.
- Confirmation has been received from the Company's bankers that a repayment holiday on loans will be
made available for 3 months initially.
- A claim for business interruption loss is being initiated under the Company's insurance policy.
- Extended payment terms have been agreed with some of the major suppliers to the Company. These have
been made possible due to the excellent supplier relations enjoyed by the Company and built up over many
years.

At present, the directors do not anticipate that an application under the Coronavirus Business Interruption
Loan Scheme will be required, but this remains a fall-back option, should the closures remain in place longer
than first anticipated.

2. ACCOUNTING POLICIES

Basis of preparing the financial statements
These financial statements have been prepared in accordance with Financial Reporting Standard 102 "The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland" and the Companies Act 2006. The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost convention as modified by the revaluation of certain assets.

Related party exemption
The company has taken advantage of exemption, under the terms of Financial Reporting Standard 102
'The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland', not to disclose related
party transactions with wholly owned subsidiaries within the group.

Turnover
Turnover represents net invoiced sale of goods, excluding value added tax. The company's policy is to
recognise a sale when substantively all the risks and rewards in connection with the goods have been
passed to the buyer. For properties held for rental purposes, rental income is included at the point of
invoice.

Goodwill
Goodwill has arisen from the acquisition of businesses in 2013, 2017 and 2018 and is being written off
over 10 years on a straight line basis.

Intangible assets
Intangible assets are initially measured at cost. After initial recognition, intangible assets are measured
at cost less any accumulated amortisation and any accumulated impairment losses.

Tenants' Leases is being amortised evenly over its estimated useful life of ten years.

Tangible fixed assets
Depreciation is provided at the following annual rates in order to write off each asset over its
estimated useful life, or if held under a finance lease, over the lease term, whichever is shorter.

Heritable properties- 2% and 4% on cost, where the residual value is assessed to be below the
current net book value
Fixtures and fittings- 20% on cost
Motor vehicles- 25% on cost
Computer equipment- 25% on cost

Heritable properties are being held under the revaluation model. All other tangible fixed assets are held
under the cost model.

The directors believe that the residual values of heritable properties will match or exceed their current
net book values. Therefore no depreciation has been provided in the current year.

Leasehold property is depreciated on a straight line basis over the duration of the lease.

2. ACCOUNTING POLICIES - continued

Investment property
Investment property is shown at most recent valuation. The properties are assessed for any
impairment of their carrying value and where required the valuation is adjusted for impairment. Any
aggregate surplus or deficit arising from changes in fair value is recognised in profit or loss.

Where a fair value adjustment has been made, the amount has been transferred from retained
earnings to a fair value reserve in order to distinguish between distributable and non distributable
reserves.

Depreciation shown against investment properties is accumulated depreciation which was held against
the properties at the the point they were reclassified as investment properties. No further depreciation
has been recognised since that date.

Stocks
Stocks are valued at the lower of cost and net realisable value, after making due allowance for
obsolete and slow moving items.

Financial instruments
The Company only enters into basic financial instrument transactions that result in the recognition of
financial assets and liabilities like trade and other accounts receivable and payable, loans from banks
and other third parties.

Debt instruments (other than those wholly repayable or receivable within one year), including loans and
other accounts receivable and payable are initially measured at present value of future cash flows and
subsequently amortised at cost using the effective interest method. Where loans have been
consolidated, this has been treated as a new loan agreement and the effective rate of interest has
been recalculated.

Creditors payable within one year, typically trade payables, are measured, initially and subsequently at
the undiscounted amount of the cash or other consideration expected to be paid.

Debtors payable within one year, typically trade receivables, are measured initially and subsequently at
the undiscounted amount of the cash or other consideration expected to be received.

Taxation
Taxation for the year comprises current and deferred tax. Tax is recognised in the Income Statement,
except to the extent that it relates to items recognised in other comprehensive income or directly in
equity.

Current or deferred taxation assets and liabilities are not discounted.

Current tax is recognised at the amount of tax payable using the tax rates and laws that have been
enacted or substantively enacted by the statement of financial position date.

Deferred tax
Deferred tax is recognised in respect of all timing differences that have originated but not reversed at
the statement of financial position date.

Timing differences arise from the inclusion of income and expenses in tax assessments in periods
different from those in which they are recognised in financial statements. Deferred tax is measured
using tax rates and laws that have been enacted or substantively enacted by the year end and that are
expected to apply to the reversal of the timing difference.

Unrelieved tax losses and other deferred tax assets are recognised only to the extent that it is probable
that they will be recovered against the reversal of deferred tax liabilities or other future taxable profits.

Hire purchase and leasing commitments
