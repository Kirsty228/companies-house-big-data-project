Objectives and activities

The Ecology Centre’s mission is to provide direct experience of the environment to help people improve their quality of life. The Centre’s vision is of an aware society at the heart of a sustainable environment.

 

Our aims are:

 

•    To provide first class environmental experiences, tailored to the needs of those who come here to learn and participate.

•    To improve health and wellbeing, increase individuals’ skills and encourage social contact by providing volunteering experiences for people with a range of backgrounds.

•    To strengthen the sense of community through events and activities.

 

Significant activities

 

Our services include:

  • Environmental education for nursery and school children, youth organisations, community groups and those with challenging behavior.

  • Outdoor volunteering opportunities for a wide variety of people including unemployed people, individuals with disabilities, trainees, students and people with mental health problems.

  • Tool shed volunteering to refurbish tools that are then donated to good causes at home and abroad, including sessions for people living with dementia.

  • Development and maintenance of grounds by Kinghorn Loch as a community resource, to encourage and enable outdoor recreational activities, such as walking, cycling and picnicking.

  • Community events and workshops to encourage the local community to be involved at the Centre, learn new skills and enjoy the environment.

  • Citizen Science, involving the community in wildlife monitoring.

  • Our Bright Future Modern Apprenticeships in Estate Maintenance, offering placements for young people to learn skills in environmental work and gain experience and confidence to enhance their opportunities in the workplace.

  • Delivery of community and education projects in partnership with others.

 

These services are delivered mainly at the Kinghorn Loch site, but some outreach work also takes place.

Achievements and performance

 

Charitable activities

 

During the period from July 2018 to June 2019, The Ecology Centre’s Education programme worked with 8,152 individuals.

A total of 3 work placements were supported during 2018-19.

 

Summary of 2018-19

In 2018-19, The Ecology Centre celebrated the 20th year of its existence, marking the occasion with a series of events and activities through 2018, including our biggest ever Summer Festival, a ceilidh for past and present volunteers, and attending Scottish Parliament to witness the passing of a special motion to mark the Centre’s contribution towards human and environmental matters over the last two decades.

 

It was also a major milestone in reach for the Centre, with the completion of a five-year project to create a purpose-built facility, offices and site to host all of the charity’s varied programmes of work, funded by The National Lottery Community Fund’s Growing Community Assets II programme. The executive committee, staff and volunteers of The Ecology Centre would like to place on record their appreciation to The National Lottery Community Fund for their strong support. Our work to develop the site further to encourage greater biodiversity and engage with visitors about environmental matters will continue beyond this project, but it has given us an excellent platform from which to deliver.

PLANS FOR THE FUTURE

 

Education

 

In light of the sustained demand for our environmental education services, the decision was made to expand our staffing team at the end of the year and we began an extensive recruitment process to recruit 2 new Education Officers. These staff members were bedded in over 2018/19 as we look to increase our capacity over the coming year.

 

Practices also continue to develop as we establish a greater emphasis on emotional support for children and young people as part of our education offer to partner schools. With progress being made by staff, trainees and volunteers on development of our site, the focus of the Education team will be on accessing and enhancing local greenspaces for groups of schoolchildren we work with.

 

Volunteering

 

The success of our new volunteering projects means that they now sit alongside our existing activities to provide a myriad of opportunities through the working week. Our main volunteering day on Wednesdays draws a significant number of people to undertake conservation work onsite and we will continue to develop our land to make it as accessible and welcoming for people and flora and fauna alike, to encourage people to reap the benefits of spending more time in nature.

 

Our tool shed has expanded to fill its current capacity and operates with an ongoing waiting list for potential new volunteers to join us. With this in mind, we will continue to develop the ‘Wide Open Doors’ project to offer alternative activities for people living with dementia and to improve our facilities and staff training to make The Ecology Centre a truly dementia-friendly destination in Fife.

 

Our community garden continues to expand, led by a staff community gardener. In collaboration with Kinghorn Community Land Association, we are developing what was previously a field left to pasture into a productive food growing area, the produce of which will be prepared and cooked into healthy meals for our volunteers and visitors.

 

Work placements

 

We will continue with our training programme of Modern Apprenticeships in Estate Maintenance through the ‘Our Bright Future’ project, delivered in cooperation with our fellow members of Fife Rural Skills Partnership. Work placements and training are also set to continue through opportunities funded by Community Jobs Scotland.

 

Enterprise

 

The focus of our social enterprise activities in 2018-19, was a conscious move away from the provision of expensive wooden products and corporate volunteering opportunities, towards emphasis of on venue hire, events and catering. In October 2017 we held our first wedding onsite and will be looking to develop our offer for events like these going forward.

Recruitment and appointment of new trustees

 

Trustees are recruited to ensure representation of a broad and appropriate range of skills, experience and qualities, to create an effective governing Board. Applicants go through a formal recruitment process and are appointed for a year. Those wishing to stand for another term present themselves for re-election at the AGM.

Organisational Structure

 

Full membership of The Ecology Centre is open to all those living in defined postcodes of Kinghorn, Burntisland, Aberdour and Auchtertool areas. Full members are eligible to attend AGMs and take part in decision-making over the running and future of the Centre. Those based outside this radius may join as associate members, but do not have voting rights.

 

The Executive Committee is responsible for governance and the setting of policy and strategy. Day to day management is the responsibility of the staff. The General Manager heads the organisation and reports directly to the Executive Committee at their meetings every 6 weeks (or more regularly as required). The General Manager acts as Company Secretary.

 

The internal management team – made up of the General Manager, Education Manager, Marketing Manager, Site Manager and Centre Assistant deal with the day-to-day management of the organisation.

 

As of 30 June 2019, The Ecology Centre employed 13 members of staff (approximately 7 full-time equivalents). Staff resources are enhanced with employment of education staff on a seasonal basis and young people on work placements.

1.10
Financial instruments

The charity has elected to apply the provisions of Section 11 ‘Basic Financial Instruments’ and Section 12 ‘Other Financial Instruments Issues’ of FRS 102 to all of its financial instruments.

 

Financial instruments are recognised in the charity's balance sheet when the charity becomes party to the contractual provisions of the instrument.

 

Financial assets and liabilities are offset, with the net amounts presented in the financial statements, when there is a legally enforceable right to set off the recognised amounts and there is an intention to settle on a net basis or to realise the asset and settle the liability simultaneously.

Basic financial assets

Basic financial assets, which include debtors and cash and bank balances, are initially measured at transaction price including transaction costs and are subsequently carried at amortised cost using the effective interest method unless the arrangement constitutes a financing transaction, where the transaction is measured at the present value of the future receipts discounted at a market rate of interest. Financial assets classified as receivable within one year are not amortised.

Basic financial liabilities

Basic financial liabilities, including creditors and bank loans are initially recognised at transaction price unless the arrangement constitutes a financing transaction, where the debt instrument is measured at the present value of the future payments discounted at a market rate of interest. Financial liabilities classified as payable within one year are not amortised.

 

Debt instruments are subsequently carried at amortised cost, using the effective interest rate method.

 

Trade creditors are obligations to pay for goods or services that have been acquired in the ordinary course of operations from suppliers. Amounts payable are classified as current liabilities if payment is due within one year or less. If not, they are presented as non-current liabilities. Trade creditors are recognised initially at transaction price and subsequently measured at amortised cost using the effective interest method.

Derecognition of financial liabilities

Financial liabilities are derecognised when the charity’s contractual obligations expire or are discharged or cancelled.

PURPOSE OF RESTRICTED FUNDS

 

Big Lottery Health Check

 

Big Lottery Scotland provided Health Check funding to support the increase in marketing and promotional activity of the centre as we seek to increase income generating activities and reduce our reliance on grant funding streams. It funded the development and implementation of our new website and contributed towards the salary costs of the Marketing Manager.

 

Community Works & Community Works II

 

Grants from Fife Council Community Works programme assisted us and our trainees with the development of landscaping and facilities on the new site.

 

Common Ground

 

Donation provided to support Tool Shed supplies for refurbished tools being send overseas.

 

Edina Trust

 

The Edina Trust supported a new education programme focussed on the Fife shoreline: Coastal Classrooms and Seashore Science.

 

Enterprise Ready Fund

 

The Enterprise Ready Fund provided funding for the purchase of resources to support income generation activities, including our Eco Events programme.

 

Fit for Purpose

 

Climate Challenge Fund from the Scottish Government supported our Lemon Squeezy project in 2016 and the Fit For Purpose project in 2017, following on from the success of 2015’s Easy Peas-y project to encourage our community to grow its own vegetables and reduce the purchase of produce with high food miles. The fund also supported the development and implementation of our Tool Shed, where volunteers renovate old tools to stop them from going to landfill. Some of the tools are collated into kits and sent overseas to help people in developing countries to set up their own small businesses.

 

Scottish Natural Heritage

 

The Ecology Centre education and volunteering programme was supported by Scottish Natural Heritage as the second part of a two year programme. The Centre is extremely grateful for receiving 15 years of continuous support from Scottish Natural Heritage for a variety of activities which help people connect with the natural world.

 

Lloyds TSB

 

Grant funding from Lloyds TSB supported the implementation of our dementia-friendly tool shed, funding the salaries of the tool shed officer and a support worker to run sessions every Thursday morning.
